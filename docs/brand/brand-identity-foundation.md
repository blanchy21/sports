## The One Word

**EARN**

X gives you a platform. Medium gives you an audience. Socios gives you a badge. SportsBlock gives you money. That single word separates every competitor from everything SportsBlock is building.

---

## 1. Brand Archetype

**Primary: The Outlaw — Secondary: The Hero**

The Outlaw archetype is the only one that fits SportsBlock perfectly. The entire value proposition is built on rejecting the existing order:

- X takes your content and profits from it — you don't
- Medium gatekeeps monetisation behind subscriber thresholds
- Socios sells the illusion of ownership

SportsBlock blows that up and hands earnings directly back to the fan.

The Outlaw says: *"the system is rigged against you — here's the exit."*

The Hero secondary layer is essential because Outlaws without a mission become nihilistic. Users aren't just rejecting platforms — they're **winning**. They're proving their sports knowledge is worth real money. The Hero craves mastery and reward through merit, and prediction markets deliver exactly that.

**Brand language that reflects this archetype:**
- "Pure Sports. Real Rewards." — Outlaw
- "You own everything." — Outlaw
- "Zero fees." — Outlaw
- "Your hot take. Your stake. Your payout." — Hero

## 3. Core Brand Promise

> **Your sports knowledge is worth real money — SportsBlock is the only place on earth that proves it.**

This promise works on three levels simultaneously:
1. It validates the user's identity as a knowledgeable fan
2. It creates a direct challenge — "prove it"
3. It frames SportsBlock as the singular, exclusive destination

The word "proves" is deliberate — it activates the prediction mechanic without explaining it technically.

---

## 4. The 3 Emotions — In Order, Every Time

Every time someone encounters SportsBlock — ad, tweet, onboarding screen, notification — they should feel these three emotions in sequence:

| Step | Emotion | What triggers it |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Disruption** | "Wait — they actually pay me for this?" |
| 2 | **Hunger** | "I know this game better than anyone. I'm going all in." |
| 3 | **Ownership** | "This is my platform. My earnings. My tribe." |

Disruption breaks existing beliefs about platforms. Hunger activates competitive identity. Ownership creates long-term loyalty that no traditional platform can replicate — because no traditional platform gives users anything to own.

## 5. Brand Voice Guide

### Sound like this ✓

- "Back your call. Earn your MEDALS. Simple."
- "You called it. The blockchain remembers."
- "Pure sports. Zero noise. Real earnings."
- "Your hot take. Your stake. Your payout."

### Never sound like this ✗

- "Our innovative platform leverages blockchain technology to incentivise..."
- "We are committed to creating a safe and inclusive space for..."
- "Please note that cryptocurrency carries risk and..."
- "Our community guidelines prohibit content that..."

### Voice Rules

- Short sentences win
- Never explain the tech
- Sports vernacular first
- Active voice always
- Challenge the user
- No corporate hedging
- `MEDALS` is always capitalised
- No politics — not even by implication

## 6. Competitive Differentiation

| Competitor | What they offer fans | What SportsBlock offers instead |
|------------|---------------------|--------------------------------|
| **X (Twitter)** | Reach. Noise. Ad revenue for a tiny elite. Political chaos. Your content feeds their algorithm for free. | MEDALS for every prediction that lands. You own your content permanently on Hive. Zero political bleed. |
| **Medium** | Long-form writing for a writing audience. Paywalled monetisation requiring subscriber scale you probably don't have. | No writing required. A hot take is enough. Every sports fan qualifies on day one. |
| **Socios** | Fan tokens that let you vote on trivial club decisions. Passive holding. Value driven by club marketing, not your knowledge. | MEDALS earned through accuracy. Your ROI tied to your sports IQ, not a club's PR calendar. |

**The fundamental gap no competitor closes:** none of them pay fans for being right about sport. That gap is the entire SportsBlock positioning.
